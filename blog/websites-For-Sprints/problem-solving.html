    How confident I am with these following problem-solving techniques.

  • Pseudo-code

    • I am very confident with Pseudocode as being able to write down fake code and work from there gives me clear visual understanding of what I am doing.

  • Trying something

    • Trying something is the problem-solving method I use the most. Through multiple attempts and errors I am able to gain more understanding of the topic or question.

  • Rubber ducky method

    • I find the rubber ducky method very effective as it makes me question my codes purpose or use. But I realize I need to put more effort into applying that method.

  • Reading error messages

    • If I come across an error message I am less confident in being able to understand what the error message is portraying or saying is wrong. I need to invest more time in understand what exactly an error message means.

  • console.logging

    • I use console.logging as one of my main sources of problem solving as it gives me a clear visual image of what my code is doing.

    • I struggle with asking peers for help more than any other problem-solving method. This is because its something I have always struggled to do. I realize this is something to work on as it plays a vital role in making sure the team I am in is successful.

  • Asking coaches for help
