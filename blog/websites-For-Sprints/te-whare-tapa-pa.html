Taha Tiana

  • What is Taha Tiana

    • Taha Tinana refers to the physical dimension of wellbeing in Maori culture. It encompasses all aspects of the body, including fitness, nutrition, sleep, and overall health. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining a healthy and balanced lifestyle to promote physical wellbeing, which is seen as interconnected with mental, emotional, and spiritual wellbeing. In Maori culture, Taha Tinana is viewed as a foundational aspect of overall wellbeing and is considered essential for leading a fulfilling and meaningful life.

    Taha Whanau

  • What is Taha Whanau

    • Taha Whanau refers to the holistic concept of family wellbeing in Māori culture, encompassing the physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual aspects of each family member's health. It recognizes the interconnectedness and interdependence of individuals within the family unit and emphasizes the importance of maintaining strong relationships, cultural traditions, and community support for promoting family wellbeing. Overall, Taha Whanau embodies a holistic approach to health and wellbeing that values the collective strength and resilience of the family.

  • How I will use Taha Whanau in my wellbeing plan.

    • I will incorporate Taha Whanau into my wellbeing plan by making sure I keep in touch with my family members every day so I can utuilize their combined strength to help me with my stressful and anxious moments.

    Taha Hinengaro

  • Tahe Hinengaro meaning

    • Taha Hinengaro is a concept that originates from the Māori culture of New Zealand and refers to the dimension of emotional and mental well-being. In Māori culture, health and well-being are seen as holistic, encompassing the physical, spiritual, social, and mental aspects of a person's life. Taha Hinengaro specifically refers to the mind, thoughts, and emotions of an individual. Taha Hinengaro is closely linked to the concept of "whakapapa," which refers to the interconnectedness of all things in the universe. In this context, emotional and mental well-being is seen as being intimately connected to a person's sense of identity, sense of purpose, and connection to their ancestors and the natural world. Taha Hinengaro encompasses a wide range of mental states, from feeling calm and content to experiencing anxiety, depression, and other forms of distress. It is believed that a person's emotional and mental state can be influenced by a range of factors, including their relationships, environment, cultural background, and life experiences. Overall, the concept of Taha Hinengaro emphasizes the importance of emotional and mental well-being as a key component of overall health and wellness. It highlights the need for individuals to take care of their mental health and seek support when needed to maintain balance and harmony in their lives.

I will have 20 minutes relaxing and enjoying myself so I have the mental energy to take on the next day and appericiate the day I just had.

    Taha Wairua

    Taha Wairua refers to the spiritual dimension of health and wellbeing, which encompasses an individual's relationship with themselves, others, and the wider world. It involves a sense of purpose and meaning in life, as well as a connection to one's cultural and spiritual beliefs, practices, and traditions. For Maori, spirituality is an integral part of their culture and identity, and Taha Wairua is considered essential for achieving overall wellbeing. It is believed that a strong and healthy spirit is necessary to maintain balance and harmony within oneself, with others, and with the environment. Taha Wairua can be nurtured through various practices and rituals, such as prayer, meditation, or participating in cultural ceremonies and traditions. It can also be supported by engaging in activities that promote a sense of purpose, meaning, and connection, such as volunteering, spending time in nature, or pursuing creative passions.

    Whenua

  • What is Whenua

    • Whenua is a Maori term that encompasses a deep sense of connection to the land and the environment. It is not just a physical connection but also a spiritual and cultural connection that the Maori people have with the natural world. Whenua is a term that describes the interdependence of all things in the environment, including the land, water, plants, animals, and people. It acknowledges that the land is not just a resource to be exploited but also a source of spiritual nourishment and cultural identity. For the Maori people, the whenua is a living entity with its own mana, or power. It is seen as the foundation of all life and the source of all sustenance. The Maori people have a deep respect for the whenua and have developed a complex set of customs and practices to ensure its protection and preservation.

  • How I will incorporate Whenua

    • My goal with incorporating Whenua into my wellbeing plan is to make sure I go for a walk or hike in nature at least once every two weeks.
