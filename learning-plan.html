Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.


One situation where I had to make an ethical decision was when I worked at a retail store and a customer tried to return a used item without a receipt. While the store policy allowed for returns without receipts, it was clear that the item had been worn and was not in its original condition. I had to weigh up the value s of honesty, fairness, and the importance of following company policies. After considering these values, I decided to politely explain to the customer that we could not accept the return as it was against store policy. Looking back on the decision, I feel that I made the right choice as it was important to uphold the store's policies and maintain fairness for all customers.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.


An example of when I faced resistance while working with others was during a group project in college. I wanted to delegate tasks and set deadlines, but some group members were not interested in following through or were resistant to taking on specific tasks. To address the tension, I held a group meeting to discuss each member's strengths and weaknesses and allocated tasks accordingly. I also set clear deadlines and regularly checked in with everyone's progress. While this strategy helped to some extent, there was still some tension and procrastination from a few group members. Looking back, I think I could have tried to understand the underlying reasons for the resistance and worked with the group members to find solutions that would work.
